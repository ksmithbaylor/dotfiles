Access Local Network Resources (Suspend/Resume Connection)

When you have an active VPN connection, you might not be able to access local network resource such as printers and shared drives. You can suspend your VPN connection to connect to local resources and then reactivate the VPN connection to the remote network without having to provide login credentials.

Note: When you suspend a connection, you no longer have access to the network resources provided by that connection. For example, you might lose connectivity to your corporate email system and applications that require authenticated network access. When you resume the Pulse connection, the network resources provided by the Pulse connection are restored.


Note: If the Pulse administrator has enabled local subnet access, you can access resources, such as a printer, that is connected to the same subnet as your computer without suspending the Pulse connection.

To suspend the VPN connection and access local network resources:

  1. On the menu bar, click File > Connections > Suspend.

    Note that the Suspend connection option is not available if you choose to Forget Saved Settings while you have an active connection.

To reactivate a suspended VPN connection:

  1. On the menu bar, click File > Connections > Resume.
