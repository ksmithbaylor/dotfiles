Remove Manual Override on an Automatic Connection

Your Pulse administrator can provide Pulse connections that connect automatically. For example, you might have one Pulse connection that activates when you are in the office and connected to the LAN, and a different Pulse connection that activates when you are away from the office. Automatic connections simplify connectivity by limiting manual connection decisions. Your Pulse administrator can allow you to override automatic connections.

If you manually connect or disconnect an automatic connection, that connection loses its ability to behave automatically until you either restart Pulse or remove the manual override on the connection. Even if you manually reconnect, the connection can no longer respond automatically. Pulse displays manual override for automatic connections that no longer respond automatically.

To restore automatic behavior to a connection that has been set to manual override:

  1. On the Connections pane, click the connection to select it.
  2. On the menu bar, click File > Connections > Remove Override (return to automatic control).
